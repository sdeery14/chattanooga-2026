# Bouldering Areas: Chattanooga Region

> Quick reference for area selection. All drive times from Enterprise South Nature Park area (lodging).
> Trip dates: February 14–21, 2026.
> **Maps**: Interactive maps below render on GitHub web. If using the GitHub mobile app, open in your phone's browser instead.

## Tier 1: Primary Destinations

High-quality hard bouldering with deep problem counts. These are the areas most likely to anchor the trip.

- [Stone Fort (LRC)](stone-fort.md)
- [Rocktown](rocktown.md)
- [Horse Pens 40 (HP40)](hp40.md)

## Tier 2: Supplementary Areas

Worth visiting for specific purposes — half-day sessions, pairing with a Tier 1 day, or moderate-focused climbing.

- [Dayton Pocket](dayton-pocket.md)
- [Zahnd Tract](zahnd-tract.md)
- [Dogwood](dogwood.md)
- [Hospital Boulders](hospital-boulders.md)
- [Walden's Ridge Park](waldens-ridge.md)
- [Cumberland Boulders / Pep Boys](cumberland-pep-boys.md)

## Tier 3: Brief Profiles

Included for completeness. Not primary bouldering destinations for this trip.

- [Sunset Rock](sunset-rock.md) — primarily trad climbing
- [Hell's Kitchen](hells-kitchen.md) — developing; thin public info
- [Foster Falls](foster-falls.md) — sport climbing area; only 6 boulder problems
- [Suck Creek](suck-creek.md) — hard area (V5–V14); poor February conditions

## Quick Decision Filters

**Best for V7–V10 climbers**: HP40 > Rocktown > Stone Fort

**Best for the whole group**: Stone Fort (deepest easy + moderate pool) > Rocktown

**Closest to lodging**: Walden's Ridge (~25 min) > Sunset Rock (~20 min, but not a bouldering destination)

**Best if conditions are bad (humid/warm)**: Higher elevation areas — Rocktown (~2,200 ft) > Stone Fort (~1,800 ft)

**Best for a half-day session**: Walden's Ridge (close + no fee) or Dayton Pocket (pair with another area)

**Best rest-day addon**: Zahnd (pair with Rocktown, 15 min apart)

**Stone Fort rain-day alternative**: Cumberland / Pep Boys (same mountain, ~5–10 min away, dries faster)

**Skip in February**: Suck Creek (high water, submerged landings, cold creek crossings)
